Software Engineering is an extremely enjoyable and useful course. We were taught tangible skills on how to build great software that both users and developers will love. Independent Work

Operating Systems was a very enjoyable module, because I have always marvelled at how a single piece of software can be used by so many different people for extremely different purposes. It taught me the basics, and left me wanting for more, and I partially satisfy my curiosity by consulting books on Linux development.

My focus area of choice is Parallel Systems, because I believe that is the future. A single processor is now limited by the laws of physics, and the way to make our computers run faster is to run programs in parallel. My experience taking those two modules, Parallel Computing and Parallel and Concurrent Programming, has been extremely fruitful, opening my eyes to the world of parallel systems, MPI, multithreading, model checking and CSP, network topology, amongst many other things.

The Independent Module spanned the months of May, June and July of 2012. Teams paired up and were allowed to work on any project they want. My partner and I developed a game, called Snowball. This was challenging because we had little guidance, unlike other modules where there were lectures and tutorial, and that forced us to research and learn a lot. Game development is also a new thing for both of us, and it was a rewarding challenge, because at the end of this module, what we have built came solely from us.
